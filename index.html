Key definitions (Secure Data Networks)

6G: The next generation beyond 5G, targeted for the 2030s, with security built‑in by design: zero‑trust principles, end‑to‑end protection with post‑quantum readiness, secure network slicing, sensing‑communication convergence, and native interworking with non‑terrestrial links.

Standards & timeline (high level)

  • 3GPP Release 18: start of 5G‑Advanced with enhancements that bridge toward 6G capabilities.
  • 3GPP Release 19: continues 5G‑Advanced and explores pre‑6G, including security studies and NTN evolution.
  • 6G studies: SA2/SA3 are defining 6G architecture and security items (e.g., authentication models, quantum‑safe migration).

NTN (Non‑Terrestrial Networks): Integration of satellites and high‑altitude platforms with 3GPP networks to extend coverage and backhaul while reusing authentication, key management, and roaming from the mobile ecosystem; focus areas include jamming resilience, secure beams/handovers, and efficient cryptography under tight link budgets.

NTN spans spaceborne (LEO/MEO/GEO) and airborne (HAPS, UAVs) platforms to cover oceans, rural areas, and disaster zones and to provide direct‑to‑device (D2D/D2C) services. 3GPP added normative NTN support in Release 17 for NR and IoT, with further enhancements in Releases 18/19.
